The Twelve Hallmarks of Aging

Unified framework describing the primary molecular and cellular processes that drive aging across species. Updated 2023 revision expands original nine hallmarks to twelve based on accumulated evidence.

03 â€” Historical Context

Longevity Research Timeline

Key discoveries and milestones in the scientific understanding of aging biology and development of lifespan-extending interventions.

1935

Caloric Restriction Discovery

Clive McCay demonstrates that dietary restriction extends lifespan in rats by ~30%. First reproducible intervention to slow aging, establishing nutrition as key factor.

McCay CM et al. J Nutr. 1935;10(1):63-79
1961

Hayflick Limit

Leonard Hayflick discovers human cells can only divide ~50 times before senescence. Challenges prevailing immortality dogma, establishes cellular aging as intrinsic process.

Hayflick L, Moorhead PS. Exp Cell Res. 1961;25:585-621
1993

First Longevity Gene (daf-2)

Cynthia Kenyon's lab shows C. elegans daf-2 mutants (insulin/IGF-1 pathway) live 2x longer. Proves aging is genetically regulated, not inevitable decay.

Kenyon C et al. Nature. 1993;366(6454):461-464
2006

Yamanaka Factors (iPSCs)

Shinya Yamanaka reprograms adult cells to pluripotent state using four factors (OSKM). Nobel Prize 2012. Foundation for cellular rejuvenation strategies.

Takahashi K, Yamanaka S. Cell. 2006;126(4):663-676
2009

Rapamycin Extends Mouse Lifespan

First FDA-approved drug shown to extend lifespan in mammals (+9-14% in mice). mTOR pathway becomes central therapeutic target. ITP trials establish protocol.

Harrison DE et al. Nature. 2009;460(7253):392-395
2013

Hallmarks of Aging Framework

LÃ³pez-OtÃ­n et al. publish unified framework of nine biological hallmarks. Becomes foundational organizational structure for aging biology field.

2013

Horvath Epigenetic Clock

Steve Horvath develops DNA methylation-based age predictor accurate to Â±3.6 years. Enables measurement of biological vs chronological age, intervention testing.

Horvath S. Genome Biol. 2013;14(10):R115
2015

First Senolytic Drugs

Dasatinib + Quercetin combination selectively kills senescent cells in mice, extends healthspan. Opens therapeutic avenue for SASP-driven pathologies.

Zhu Y et al. Aging Cell. 2015;14(4):644-658
2016

In Vivo Partial Reprogramming

Izpisua Belmonte's lab demonstrates transient OSKM expression reverses aging hallmarks in progeroid mice without teratoma formation.

Ocampo A et al. Cell. 2016;167(7):1719-1733
2020

NAD+ Restoration via NMN

Multiple groups show NMN supplementation reverses vascular aging and improves exercise capacity in aged mice. Human trials begin showing biomarker improvements.

Das A et al. Cell. 2018;173(1):74-89
2020

Cellular Rejuvenation via mRNA

Sinclair lab demonstrates age reversal in mouse retinal neurons using OSK (without Myc) delivered via AAV gene therapy. Vision restoration in glaucoma model.

Lu Y et al. Nature. 2020;588(7836):124-129
2021

Altos Labs Founded

$3B cellular rejuvenation company launched with Yamanaka, Izpisua Belmonte, and others. Largest private investment in aging biology to date. Commercial validation of field.

Updated framework expands to twelve hallmarks, adding disabled macroautophagy, chronic inflammation, and dysbiosis based on decade of accumulated research.

Multiple Phase II senolytic trials ongoing (D+Q, fisetin). Partial reprogramming approaches expected to enter human trials 2025-2026. TAME metformin trial enrolling. About VECVO

VECVO is a research compilation project focused on the biology of aging and lifespan-extending interventions. The project synthesizes peer-reviewed literature, ongoing clinical trials, and mechanistic research into the twelve hallmarks of aging.

All content is referenced to primary literature. Evidence strength is explicitly indicated using standardized categories: high (multiple RCTs or large cohorts), moderate (observational studies or limited trials), low (animal models only), preliminary (early-stage research). Speculative content is labeled as such.

The project prioritizes honest assessment of translational barriers, uncertainty acknowledgment, and distinction between established findings and ongoing research. No commercial affiliations or conflicts of interest.

Methodology

Content selection follows evidence hierarchy:

  1. Human randomized controlled trials (RCTs)
  2. Large observational cohort studies (n>1,000)
  3. Mammalian model organism studies (mice, rats, primates)
  4. Short-lived model organisms (C. elegans, Drosophila)
  5. In vitro studies and cellular models
  6. Theoretical mechanisms and computational predictions

Interventions are assessed on: strength of evidence, effect size magnitude, safety profile, practical implementation feasibility, and cost-benefit ratio. Translation uncertainty from model organisms to humans is explicitly discussed for all pharmacological interventions.
